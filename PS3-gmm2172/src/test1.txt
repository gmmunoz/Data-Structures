Throughout the Oresteia, the idea that every action has a consequence is an epithet that that is consistently repeatedly throughout each of the three plays. Beginning with Clytaemestra murdering Agamemnon and as a result Orestes killing his own mother, it is apparent that each unjust deed must be made right via punishment or sacrifice. After Orestes has committed murder, he is then sent to have a trial with Athena and twelve jury members. This trial serves not only to determine whether Orestes killed his mother but also analyze whether the murders he committed were just or unjust. Prior to to trial, Orestes goes to pray in front of the statue of Athena asking for aid in trial as well as help in the upcoming events he will face.
